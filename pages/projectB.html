Local Vibes

City Ratings Web App

Introducing Local Vibes, a user-friendly city ratings app designed to provide insights into cities across the United States. This database-driven platform offers real-time ratings and reviews, allowing users to explore essential factors like cost of living, crime rates, housing, employment, schools, and cultural amenities. Whether you're a traveler searching for the perfect destination or a potential resident curious about your next hometown, Local Vibes is your guide to unraveling the unique characteristics of each city. With an intuitive interface and a wealth of information, it's a go-to resource for anyone interested in discovering the essence of urban spaces.

What kind of project is this?

Local Vibes is a meticulously designed city ratings app focused on U.S. cities with populations exceeding 100,000. The database-driven architecture incorporates key entities such as Cities, Users, Ratings, Reviews, and Categories, facilitating a comprehensive exploration of urban landscapes. The database establishes 1:M connections between Cities and Reviews, Cities and Ratings, as well as M:M connections between Cities and Users, emphasizing the dynamic relationship between users and city data. The Users entity captures user information, including user_id, username, user_fname, user_lname, email, gender, and race_ethnicity, fostering 1:M relationships with Reviews and Ratings, and M:M relationships with Cities through the Users_Cities intersection table. This table manages user residencies, recording details like user_city_id, user_id, city_id, start_year, end_year, and current_resident. The Reviews entity details city reviews, while the Ratings entity stores category-specific scores, both forming connections with Cities and Users. Categories entity defines rating and review criteria, establishing a M:1 relationship with Ratings. Local Vibes seamlessly integrates a sophisticated database structure, providing users with a technically robust platform for insightful exploration of city characteristics.


Key Entities

  • Cities: US cities with populations over 100,000.
  • Users: Individuals interacting with the platform.
  • Ratings: Numerical evaluations based on criteria like cost of living, crime rate, housing, employment, schools, culture/amenities, and user ratings.
  • Reviews: Detailed feedback and opinions posted by users, similar to platforms like IMDb or Rotten Tomatoes.
  • Categories: Criteria used for city ratings.



Sample Database With Normalization Applied



USERS

  • 1:M Users to Reviews: One user can leave zero, one, or many reviews
  • 1:M Users to Ratings: One user can contribute to many ratings
  • M:M Users to Cities: One user can be a past/present resident of many cities, and one city can have many users (residents)
  • 1:M Users to Users_Cities: One user can have many residencies, defining the M:M relationship of Users with Cities


CITIES

  • 1:M Cities to Reviews: One city can have many reviews
  • 1:M Cities to Ratings: One city can have many ratings
  • M:M Cities to Users: One city can have many users (past/current residents) and one user can be a past/current resident of many cities
  • 1:M Cities to Users_Cities: One city can have many residencies, defining the M:M relationship of Cities with Users


USERS_CITIES

  • M:1 Users_Cities to Users: Many residencies correspond to one user
  • M:1 Users_Cities to Cities: Many residencies correspond to one city


REVIEWS

  • M:1 Reviews to Cities: Many reviews can be written for one city
  • M:1 Reviews to Users: Many reviews can be written by one user


RATINGS

  • 1:M Ratings to Categories: One rating has many categories
  • M:1 Ratings to Cities: Many ratings can be submitted for one city
  • M:1 Ratings to Users: Many ratings can be submitted by one user


CATEGORIES

  • M:1 Categories to Ratings: Many categories belong to one rating
